Hevy

LiftShift gives you analytics Hevy’s built-in charts don’t offer — muscle heatmaps, plateau detection, set-by-set feedback, and AI-ready exports. Here’s how to connect your Hevy data.

Import options

  • CSV import — Export your workout history from Hevy as a CSV file and upload it to LiftShift. This is the fastest method and works for everyone.
  • Sync methods — If you use a supported sync method, LiftShift can pull your data directly. You always control what is accessed and everything stays local in your browser.

How to export your Hevy data

  1. Open the Hevy app on your phone.
  2. Go to Settings → Export Data.
  3. Choose CSV as the export format.
  4. Save or share the file to your computer.
  5. Open LiftShift, click the import button, and select the CSV file.

LiftShift reads your exercise names, set reps and weight, and workout dates from the export. All analysis runs locally in your browser — nothing is uploaded to LiftShift servers.

What LiftShift reads from your Hevy export

  • Exercise names — Normalized automatically so slight spelling differences don’t split your data.
  • Set reps and weight — Used to calculate training volume, 1RM estimates, and PRs.
  • Dates and timestamps — Used for trend charts, calendar heatmaps, and rolling window comparisons.
  • Workout notes — Preserved and displayed in your workout history.

Troubleshooting

LiftShift can’t parse workout dates

If dates aren’t recognised, it is usually caused by a non-English export format. Make sure your phone language is set to English, re-export, and try again. Hevy exports dates in the format matching your device locale.

Charts look wrong or missing data

  • Check that exercise names are consistent across your sessions. “Bench Press” and “Barbell Bench Press” may be treated as different exercises unless LiftShift normalises them.
  • Warmup sets may be included or excluded depending on your export settings. Make sure you know what Hevy exported.
  • If you have gaps in your training history (missing weeks or months), trend charts will reflect those gaps accurately.

Units look wrong (kg vs lb)

Hevy exports weights in the unit you logged them in. LiftShift detects the unit and labels charts accordingly. If you mix kg and lb across sessions for the same exercise, trend accuracy may suffer — try to stay consistent.

Combining Hevy with other apps

Switched from Strong to Hevy? Or use both? LiftShift merges data from multiple sources into one unified dashboard. Your exercise names are normalised automatically, so “Bench Press” from Hevy and “Barbell Bench” from Strong map to the same exercise. See the Supported apps page for other import guides.

What analytics you get with Hevy data

  • Muscle heatmaps with rolling 7-day windows and volume zone scoring.
  • Smart PR tracking — all-time bests, 2-month bests, and premature PR detection.
  • Training volume trends with per-exercise and per-muscle-group breakdowns.
  • Set-by-set coaching feedback across 19 scenarios for every workout.
  • Plateau detection with actionable suggestions.
  • AI-ready training export for deeper analysis.
