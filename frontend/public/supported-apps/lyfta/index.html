Lyfta is a powerful workout tracker, and LiftShift adds the analytics layer on top — muscle heatmaps, plateau detection, PR tracking, and AI-ready exports. Here’s how to connect your Lyfta data.

Import options

  • API import — Connect your Lyfta account and LiftShift pulls your data. Everything stays local in your browser.
  • CSV import — Export your workout history from Lyfta and upload to LiftShift.

How to export your Lyfta data

  1. Open the Lyfta app on your phone.
  2. Go to Settings or the Profile section.
  3. Look for Export Data or Download History.
  4. Choose CSV as the export format.
  5. Save or share the file to your computer.
  6. Open LiftShift, click the import button, and select the CSV file.

If you can’t find the export option, check Lyfta’s in-app help or contact Lyfta support. Export options vary by app version.

What LiftShift reads from your Lyfta export

  • Exercise names — Normalised so variations are treated as the same movement where possible.
  • Set reps and weight — Used for training volume, 1RM estimates, and PR detection.
  • Dates and timestamps — Used for trend charts, consistency heatmaps, and rolling window analysis.

Troubleshooting

LiftShift can’t read my Lyfta export

Make sure you exported as CSV. If Lyfta only offers other formats (JSON, etc.), try a different export option or contact Lyfta support to request CSV export.

Exercise names are inconsistent

Lyfta may export exercises with different names depending on how you logged them. Try to use consistent names when logging workouts in Lyfta. LiftShift normalises common variations automatically.

Some workouts are missing from charts

Check that you selected the full date range when exporting. If your export is split across multiple files, import the most recent or combine them before importing.

Using Lyfta alongside other apps?

LiftShift can merge data from Hevy, Strong, and Lyfta into one dashboard. Exercise names are normalised across apps so your training history stays clean. See the Supported apps page for other import guides.

What analytics you get with Lyfta data

  • Muscle heatmaps — Rolling 7-day windows with volume zone scoring and per-muscle drill-down.
  • Smart PR tracking — All-time bests, 2-month bests, premature PRs, and PR drought alerts.
  • Training volume trends — Weekly, monthly, and custom date range comparisons.
  • Exercise status labels — Know at a glance which lifts are improving and which are stalling.
  • Set-by-set coaching feedback across 19 scenarios.
  • Plateau detection with specific, actionable next-session suggestions.
