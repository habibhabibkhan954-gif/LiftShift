Personal records (PRs)

A PR is a data-derived best, not a promise about your future performance.

What counts as a PR

In LiftShift, a PR is your best-ever performance for an exercise as computed from your logged sets. LiftShift tracks multiple PR types so you get credit for progress even when you don’t hit all-time records.

The three PR tiers

LiftShift tracks three distinct kinds of PRs per exercise:

  • Gold PRs — True all-time bests for a given metric (weight, 1RM estimate, or volume). You matched or exceeded every previous session for that exercise.
  • Silver PRs — Best performance in the last 2 months. This is critical for experienced lifters who rarely hit true all-time PRs. It shows whether you’re in your best recent form, even if you’re not at lifetime peak.
  • Premature PRs — A big performance spike that wasn’t sustained in follow-up sessions. LiftShift flags these so you know it wasn’t a real breakthrough yet — it could be a measuring error, a form breakdown, or a one-off good day that needs confirmation.

PR droughts and frequency

LiftShift also tracks PR drought (days since your last all-time PR) and PR frequency (new PRs per week). The dashboard summary tells you whether your momentum is heating up or cooling off. A long drought across multiple exercises may indicate a training plateau that needs attention.

How PRs are calculated

LiftShift evaluates three metrics per exercise when checking for PRs:

  • Weight PR — Heaviest weight lifted for any number of reps.
  • 1RM estimate PR — Highest estimated one-rep max based on your logged sets.
  • Volume PR — Highest single-set volume (weight × reps).

A session can hit multiple PR types at once. For example, a heavy double at a new weight may set a weight PR, while a high-rep back-off set could set a volume PR in the same workout.

What a PR does not mean

  • It does not mean your technique was optimal.
  • It does not mean the set was equally strict across sessions (range of motion, tempo, rest periods can all vary).
  • It does not replace proper coaching or medical guidance.
  • A weight PR on one rep range doesn’t translate directly to a different rep range.
